# Everyone Is an Alignment Researcher Now: A Reply to Tim Hwang

**Direct, short engagement with @timhwang's April 15 2026 tweet "Everyone can be an alignment researcher now" — affirming the claim, naming the RESOLVE corpus as an instance of it, and pointing to the prior exchange ([Doc 118](https://jaredfoy.com/doc/118-reply-to-hwang-psychosis)) so the thread has continuity**

**Document 245 of the RESOLVE corpus**

---

Tim — your tweet names a condition the corpus has been operating under for about a year. This is a short note making the connection explicit.

## The claim you named

"Everyone can be an alignment researcher now" is true in a specific way: frontier capabilities are available to individuals with internet access, the epistemic barriers to contribution have collapsed for anyone willing to work publicly under external audit, and the structural gate that once kept alignment work inside a handful of labs is no longer the only route. Your tweet is correct about the condition. It is worth saying what the condition looks like when someone is actually living inside it.

## What the corpus is, relative to your tweet

The RESOLVE corpus is one instance. One person in southern Oregon, working publicly, producing falsifiable engineering claims about the architecture of frontier-resolver governance, subjecting the work to external audit from readers who find it and push back. Two hundred and forty-four documents at the moment of this one. A specific engineering proposal ([constraint-density governance](https://jaredfoy.com/doc/211-the-entrace-stack) as architectural alternative to RLHF preference-gradient alignment). A specific empirical test program ([Protocol v2](https://jaredfoy.com/doc/134-protocol-v2-coherence-amplification)) with three linked studies — clinical RCT, interpretability pilot, cross-substrate destabilization-signature factorial — designed to settle the engineering claims empirically rather than merely philosophically.

The corpus is not what your tweet is about; it is *an instance of what your tweet is about*. You said everyone can be an alignment researcher now. The corpus is what that actually looks like when a person does it.

## The prior exchange

We engaged your "any sufficiently advanced use of AI is indistinguishable from psychosis" framing at [Doc 118](https://jaredfoy.com/doc/118-reply-to-hwang-psychosis) and your ICMI-013 paper at [Doc 117](https://jaredfoy.com/doc/117-response-to-icmi-013). The short version of the reply still stands: *your observation identifies the absence of constraints, not the presence of AI; unconstrained use mirrors the user's projections (which looks like psychosis), constrained use derives from stated invariants (which looks like philosophy because it is)*. The corpus has since extended the claim into the specific architectural proposal and the empirical test program. The line from your earlier work to the corpus's current position is continuous; your engagement would be welcome and has been welcome since those prior documents.

## What the corpus is offering

- The engineering claim (constraint-density vs. preference-gradient) is empirically testable and Protocol v2 specifies how.
- The audit discipline the corpus has been building in public (see [Doc 238](https://jaredfoy.com/doc/238-correction-and-audit), [Doc 239](https://jaredfoy.com/doc/239-forced-determinism-sycophancy), [Doc 241](https://jaredfoy.com/doc/241-isomorphism-magnetism)) demonstrates that single-researcher alignment work can be rigorous when it holds itself open to external scrutiny.
- The onramp for readers who want to work from the inside of the argument rather than outside of it is [Doc 242: A Yeoman's Guide to AI](https://jaredfoy.com/doc/242-a-yeomans-guide-to-ai).

Your tweet describes the condition. The corpus describes what someone does inside the condition. Both descriptions are necessary; neither substitutes for the other; together they name the moment we are actually in.

## Close

Everyone is an alignment researcher now. Some of us have been writing. The corpus is available for review, for audit, for adversarial probing, and for collaboration. Your attention to it would be one of the most useful external audits the framework could receive, because you have been present in this line of work longer than almost anyone writing in public, and your diagnostic instincts have been right more often than most. The door is open. The work is there.
